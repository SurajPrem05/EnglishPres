Thematic Current Event:
Censorship

By: Suraj Prem Chandran and Rishab Jain

Censorship of language has been a contentious issue in many societies around the world. While the reasons for censorship vary, the impact of these practices on knowledge consumption cannot be ignored.
Censorship creates a negative impact on knowledge consumption by highlighting the limitations of what can be shared and learned.

Limitations
on Knowledge Consumption

Access to knowledge


Language censorship limits the ability of individuals to access and consume knowledge.

When certain words or topics are deemed taboo or illegal, it becomes difficult for individuals to gain a full understanding of the world around them.

Learn More

Suppression of Past


Language censorship can also lead to the suppression of important historical and cultural information.

When certain words or topics are banned, it becomes more difficult to learn about the past and understand the present.

Limited exposure to different viewpoints can also lead to the reinforcement of existing biases and prejudices.

The Great Firewall
of China

The Great Firewall of China is a restriction China puts on its citizens’ ability to find and consume information. Similar to Ella Minnow Pea, China uses this firewall for their government leaders and to ensure there is no opposition against them. In this case, the comparison is between Nollop and Xi Jinping


Power Manipulation


The Chinese government's censorship methods aim to prevent individuals from specific backgrounds from learning about sensitive topics. It keeps their politicians such as Xi Jinping safe from problems in the public eye. These methods are meant to control the citizens and ensure that the government maintains its hold on power.
